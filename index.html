Rootkits: Malware in the Shadows

A rootkit is a stealthy type of malware that cybercriminals deploy to seize and maintain privileged control over a target computer or network. While it may present itself as a single binary, a rootkit typically consists of multiple components drivers, libraries, scripts, and back-door utilities that work together to mask processes, files, and network connections. By intercepting system calls and altering kernel routines, rootkits grant an attacker near-complete administrator-level access, enabling them to evade detection, persist through reboots, and manipulate sensitive data at will.

Invisible threats, unstoppable control.
