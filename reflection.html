Reflection

Firmware and kernel-mode rootkits force us to rethink “reinstallation” as a curethese threats can hide in UEFI modules or device firmware where even a full OS reimage won’t reach. Only hardware reflashing or, better yet, transparent, open-source firmware coupled with cryptographic verification can truly evict them. This reality challenges our assumptions about owning and trusting our devices: if the very code that boots our systems can’t be audited, how can we be sure it isn’t compromised? As defenders, we must push for reproducible build firmware projects, routine forensic firmware audits, and hardware designs that include verifiable recovery paths to reclaim control over our machines.
